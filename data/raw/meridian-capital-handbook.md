## Section 1: PTO & Leave Policies

### 1.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)

Meridian Capital Group provides all full-time employees with a flexible PTO program. PTO can be used for vacation, personal time, or short-term illness.

**PTO Accrual Schedule:**

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Accrual |
|---|---|
| 0 – 1 year | 15 days |
| 2 – 4 years | 20 days |
| 5 – 9 years | 25 days |
| 10+ years | 30 days |

PTO accrues on a monthly basis beginning on the employee's first day of employment. New employees are eligible to use accrued PTO after completing 90 days of service.

**PTO Carryover:**
Employees may carry over a maximum of 10 unused PTO days into the following calendar year. Any PTO balance exceeding 10 days as of December 31 will be forfeited. Meridian Capital Group does not pay out unused PTO upon resignation or termination unless required by applicable state law.

**PTO Request Process:**
All PTO requests must be submitted through the Workday HR portal a minimum of 5 business days in advance for absences of 3 or more consecutive days. For shorter absences, employees should notify their manager as early as possible. PTO approval is subject to business needs and manager discretion.

---

### 1.2 Sick Leave

In addition to PTO, all full-time employees receive 7 days of dedicated sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not accrue and does not carry over. It is provided in full at the start of each calendar year. Sick leave is intended for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member who is ill.

Employees absent due to illness for more than 3 consecutive days may be required to provide documentation from a licensed medical professional.

---

### 1.3 Parental Leave

Meridian Capital Group provides the following parental leave benefits to eligible full-time employees who have completed at least 12 months of continuous service:

- **Primary caregiver** — 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave
- **Secondary caregiver** — 6 weeks of fully paid parental leave

Parental leave applies to the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. Leave must be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event. Employees wishing to take parental leave should notify the People & Culture team at least 30 days in advance where possible.

Employees on parental leave will continue to receive full benefits including health insurance, retirement contributions, and equity vesting during their leave period.

---

### 1.4 Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to the following paid bereavement leave:

- **Immediate family** (spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, sibling) — 5 paid days
- **Extended family** (grandparent, grandchild, in-law, aunt, uncle) — 3 paid days
- **Close friend or colleague** — 1 paid day, subject to manager approval

Bereavement leave does not count against PTO or sick leave balances.

---

### 1.5 Jury Duty & Civic Leave

Employees summoned for jury duty will receive their full regular pay for up to 15 business days. Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons to the People & Culture team and their manager promptly upon receipt. If jury service extends beyond 15 days, the remaining period will be unpaid unless otherwise required by law.

---

### 1.6 Unpaid Leave of Absence

Employees who have exhausted all paid leave and require additional time away may request an unpaid leave of absence of up to 90 days. Requests must be submitted in writing to the People & Culture team and are subject to approval based on business needs, the employee's tenure, and performance standing. Benefits continuation during unpaid leave is subject to applicable law and plan terms.

## Section 2: Benefits & Compensation

### 2.1 Health Insurance

Meridian Capital Group offers comprehensive health insurance coverage through BlueCross BlueShield. Three plan tiers are available:

| Plan | Employee Monthly Premium | Employer Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Bronze | $45 | 80% |
| Silver | $110 | 75% |
| Gold | $220 | 70% |

Dependents may be added to any plan. Dependent coverage premiums are the employee's responsibility and are deducted from payroll on a pre-tax basis. Open enrollment occurs each year in November for coverage beginning January 1.

New employees are eligible for health insurance coverage beginning on the first day of the month following their start date.

---

### 2.2 Dental & Vision

**Dental** coverage is provided through MetLife. The company covers 100% of the employee premium for the standard dental plan, which includes two cleanings per year, X-rays, and 80% coverage on basic restorative procedures. Orthodontic coverage is available as an optional add-on at the employee's expense.

**Vision** coverage is provided through VSP. The company covers 100% of the employee premium. Coverage includes one annual eye exam and an allowance of $200 toward frames or contact lenses per calendar year.

---

### 2.3 Retirement — 401(k) Plan

Meridian Capital Group offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan administered by Fidelity Investments. Employees are eligible to enroll beginning on their first day of employment.

**Company Match:**
Meridian Capital Group matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of base salary, and 50% of contributions on the next 2% of base salary. The maximum company match is therefore 5% of base salary per year.

**Vesting Schedule:**
Company matching contributions vest on the following schedule:

| Years of Service | Vested Percentage |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 0% |
| 1 year | 25% |
| 2 years | 50% |
| 3 years | 75% |
| 4+ years | 100% |

Employees are always 100% vested in their own contributions.

---

### 2.4 Equity & Bonus Compensation

**Annual Performance Bonus:**
All full-time employees at the Analyst level and above are eligible for an annual performance bonus. Bonuses are discretionary and based on individual performance ratings, team performance, and company financial results. Target bonus ranges by level are as follows:

| Level | Target Bonus (% of Base Salary) |
|---|---|
| Analyst | 10% – 15% |
| Associate | 15% – 25% |
| Senior Associate | 20% – 30% |
| Vice President | 30% – 50% |
| Director | 40% – 60% |
| Managing Director | 50% – 100% |

Bonuses are paid in February following the performance year. Employees must be actively employed on the bonus payment date to receive a bonus.

**Restricted Stock Units (RSUs):**
Employees at the Vice President level and above are eligible to receive RSU grants as part of their total compensation package. RSUs vest over a 4-year period with a 1-year cliff, meaning 25% vests after the first year and the remaining 75% vests quarterly over the following 3 years.

---

### 2.5 Additional Benefits

**Commuter Benefits** — Employees may contribute up to $315 per month pre-tax toward transit or parking expenses through the Meridian commuter benefits program.

**Professional Development** — Each employee receives an annual learning and development budget of $2,500, which may be used for courses, certifications, conferences, or professional memberships relevant to their role. Requests must be approved by the employee's manager and submitted through Workday.

**Employee Assistance Program (EAP)** — Meridian Capital Group provides all employees and their household members with access to a confidential EAP through Lyra Health. Services include up to 12 free therapy sessions per year, financial counseling, legal consultations, and wellness coaching.

**Life Insurance** — The company provides basic life insurance coverage equal to 2x the employee's annual base salary at no cost to the employee. Supplemental life insurance is available for purchase.

**Gym & Wellness Reimbursement** — Employees are eligible for reimbursement of up to $600 per year for gym memberships, fitness classes, or wellness applications. Receipts must be submitted through Workday within 60 days of the expense.

---

### 2.6 Compensation Philosophy & Pay Cycles

Meridian Capital Group is committed to paying competitively within the financial services industry. Compensation is reviewed annually during the performance review cycle. Market benchmarking is conducted each year using third-party salary surveys including Radford and McLagan.

Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis (26 pay periods per year). Direct deposit is required for all employees. Payroll is processed through ADP.

## Section 3: Performance Reviews

### 3.1 Review Cycle

Meridian Capital Group conducts formal performance reviews twice per year:

- **Mid-Year Review** — Conducted in July. Focuses on progress against goals set at the start of the year, identifying development areas, and adjusting priorities for the second half of the year. Mid-year reviews do not result in compensation changes.
- **Annual Review** — Conducted in December. Comprehensive evaluation of full-year performance, resulting in a formal rating, compensation adjustment recommendations, and goal setting for the following year.

---

### 3.2 Performance Ratings

The annual performance review uses a 5-point rating scale:

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 — Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; demonstrates outstanding impact |
| 4 — Exceeds Expectations | Regularly exceeds expectations in key areas |
| 3 — Meets Expectations | Fully meets all role requirements and expectations |
| 2 — Partially Meets Expectations | Meets some but not all expectations; improvement required |
| 1 — Does Not Meet Expectations | Fails to meet core role requirements; immediate improvement plan required |

Employees receiving a rating of 2 or below will be placed on a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) within 30 days of the review. A PIP outlines specific, measurable improvement goals and a timeline, typically 60 to 90 days.

---

### 3.3 Review Process

The performance review process follows these steps:

1. **Self-Assessment** — Employees complete a self-assessment in Workday reflecting on their accomplishments, development areas, and goal progress.
2. **Manager Assessment** — Direct managers complete an independent assessment of the employee's performance.
3. **Calibration** — People managers meet with their peers and HR Business Partners to calibrate ratings across teams and ensure consistency.
4. **Review Meeting** — Managers deliver feedback to employees in a 1:1 meeting. The finalized review is shared in Workday following calibration.
5. **Goal Setting** — Employees and managers collaboratively set 3 to 5 SMART goals for the following year during the review meeting.

---

### 3.4 Compensation Adjustments

Merit increases resulting from the annual review are effective March 1 of the following year. Increase ranges are guided by the employee's performance rating and position within their salary band:

| Rating | Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|
| 5 — Exceptional | 6% – 10% |
| 4 — Exceeds Expectations | 4% – 6% |
| 3 — Meets Expectations | 2% – 4% |
| 2 — Partially Meets Expectations | 0% – 2% |
| 1 — Does Not Meet Expectations | 0% |

Merit increases are subject to budget availability and senior leadership approval.

---

### 3.5 Promotion Process

Promotions at Meridian Capital Group are considered annually as part of the December review cycle. To be considered for promotion, an employee must have received a rating of 4 or above in their most recent annual review and have been in their current role for a minimum of 18 months. Promotion decisions are made by the employee's manager in partnership with the HR Business Partner and senior leadership, and are subject to headcount and budget approval.

## Section 4: Code of Conduct

### 4.1 Our Values

Meridian Capital Group operates according to four core values that guide every decision we make:

- **Integrity** — We act honestly and transparently in all dealings with clients, colleagues, and regulators.
- **Excellence** — We hold ourselves to the highest standards of professional quality.
- **Accountability** — We take ownership of our actions and their outcomes.
- **Inclusion** — We foster a workplace where every person feels respected and valued.

---

### 4.2 Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict or appear to conflict with the interests of Meridian Capital Group or its clients. All employees are required to disclose potential conflicts of interest to their manager and the Compliance team. Examples include:

- Outside employment or consulting arrangements with clients or competitors
- Personal investments in securities that are actively managed by Meridian on behalf of clients
- Family or romantic relationships with direct reports, vendors, or clients

Outside business activities must be pre-approved by the Compliance team using the Outside Business Activity Request form in Workday.

---

### 4.3 Confidentiality & Data Privacy

Employees have access to sensitive client, financial, and proprietary information as part of their work. All such information must be treated as strictly confidential. Employees may not:

- Share client information with unauthorized third parties
- Use confidential information for personal financial gain
- Discuss client portfolios or transactions in public settings
- Store sensitive data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services

Violations of the firm's confidentiality policy may result in immediate termination and referral to regulatory authorities where applicable.

---

### 4.4 Anti-Harassment & Non-Discrimination

Meridian Capital Group is committed to maintaining a workplace free of harassment, discrimination, and retaliation. Harassment of any kind — including verbal, physical, or digital — based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

**Reporting:**
Employees who experience or witness harassment or discrimination should report it immediately to their HR Business Partner or through the anonymous Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-0192. Reports may also be submitted online at ethics.meridiancapitalgroup.com.

All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

---

### 4.5 Social Media Policy

Employees must exercise professional judgment when using social media in ways that reference Meridian Capital Group, its clients, or the financial services industry. Employees may not:

- Share confidential client or firm information on any social media platform
- Make statements that could be construed as investment advice or market commentary on behalf of Meridian
- Post content that disparages clients, colleagues, or competitors in a way that could damage the firm's reputation

Personal social media use during work hours should be limited and must not interfere with job responsibilities.

---

### 4.6 Disciplinary Process

Violations of the Code of Conduct are taken seriously. Depending on the severity of the violation, disciplinary action may include:

1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Performance Improvement Plan
4. Suspension with or without pay
5. Termination

Certain violations — including theft, fraud, harassment, or breach of client confidentiality — may result in immediate termination without prior warning. All disciplinary actions are documented in the employee's personnel file.

---

### 4.7 Workplace Safety

Meridian Capital Group is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment. Employees are expected to:

- Report unsafe conditions to their manager or Facilities immediately
- Follow all building emergency and evacuation procedures
- Not engage in any behavior that threatens the physical safety of others

Threats of violence or acts of violence in the workplace will result in immediate termination and may be referred to law enforcement.
